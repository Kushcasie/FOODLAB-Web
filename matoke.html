East African Style Matoke

Ingredients

  • Matoke Bananas 7 pieces (unripe green bananas)
  • Potatoes 1kg peeled and cubed
  • Onion: 1 large (finely diced)
  • Tomatoes: 3 (diced)
  • Garlic: 2 cloves (minced, optional)
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon (grated, optional)
  • Green Chili: 1 (chopped, adjust to taste, optional)
  • Coriander for garnish
  • Salt: 1 pinch (to taste)
  • Vegetable Oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Royco Cube: 1 (optional, for added flavor)

Instructions

  1. Peel the matoke bananas by cutting off the ends and making a slit along the length of each banana. Remove the peel and slice the bananas into halves or thirds.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onions and saut√© until they become translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chili (if using). Saut√© for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes to the pot. Cook until they soften and break down, forming a sauce-like consistency.
  5. Add the peeled and cubed potatoes to the pot. Stir well to coat them with the tomato mixture.
  6. Add the sliced matoke bananas to the pot. Gently stir to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Add enough water to cover the matoke and potatoes. Season with salt and add a Royco cube if desired. Bring to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes or until the matoke and potatoes are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  9. Once cooked, check the seasoning and adjust if necessary. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.
  10. Serve hot as a main dish or as a side with your favorite protein.
