Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot or sufuria, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add the crushed garlic and ginger to the pot and saut√© for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the diced stewing beef to the pot. Cook until the beef is browned on all sides.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste, salt, green cardamom pods, cumin seeds, and pilau masala spice. Cook for a few minutes to allow the spices to release their flavors.
  6. Add the beef broth and water to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the beef is tender.
  7. Once the beef is tender, add the drained basmati rice to the pot. Stir gently to combine everything.
  8. Cover the pot and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid. Avoid opening the lid frequently to ensure proper steaming of the rice.
  9. Once done, fluff the rice with a fork. Serve hot with kachumbari or your favorite side dish.
